Short-Term Outlook: Thursday - Saturday 

NWS State College Hazardous Weather Outlook
Numerous showers and thunderstorms forming along and ahead of a slow moving cold front could bring locally heavy rain of 1 to 2 inches in less than an hour this afternoon and tonight. The heavy rain may cause flooding of poor drainage areas and some small streams. Isolated occurences of brief strong wind gusts are also possible.

A cold front that was stretched across the Lower Great Lakes early this morning will drift slowly to the southeast across Pennsylvania today. As this frontal boundary lifts the very warm and moist air, numerous showers and scattered afternoon and evening thunderstorms will occur. Some of the storms will contain strong and gusty winds across the Lower Susquehanna Valley and adjacent southeastern portion half of the state. In addition,
where the storms train east across the same locations, locally very heavy rain, capable of producing flooding of some small streams and poor drainage areas, is possible over a short period of time.

An upper-level low pressure area and its associated pool of cold air aloft will drift over northern Pennsylvania or southern New York on Friday, continuing the likelihood of showers and scattered afternoon thunderstorms. As this upper-level feature exits to the east of the area, comfortable temperatures and lower humidity along with mainly rain-free conditions and a moderate northwest breeze will follow for much of the upcoming holiday weekend.
