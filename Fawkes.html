Short-Term Outlook: Thursday - Saturday

High pressure will build across the lower Susquehanna Valley over the next several days. Mostly clear skies today give way to partly cloudy conditions tonight along with increased humidity and a slight chance for some pop-up showers/thunderstorms. A back door cold front will drop down from the east/northeast later Friday bringing a slight chance for afternoon showers and thunderstorms with a marginal risk for isolated strong/sever storms over eastern and central Pennsylvania. Partly sunny to partly cloudy skies on the Fourth of July will also see a chance for afternoon/evening showers and thunderstorms across the Lancaster region.

Medium-Range: Sunday - Wednesday

An upper ridge of high pressure will drift eastwards over the course of the week. A cold front will approach Sunday into Monday and stall out in northeastern PA before lifting back northeast as a warm front. This will provide a mechanism for some afternoon/evening showers and thunderstorms Sunday and Monday. Tuesday and Wednesday will be hot and humid with a chance for afternoon/evening showers and thunderstorms.

Note: For US Eastern Time on maps and charts, subtract 4 hours (Daylight Saving Time) or 5 hours (Standard Time) from UTC / Z* timestamp on the maps and charts. Visit timeanddate.com for more time zone conversion information and additional time-related data.

*UTC / Z / GMT = Coordinated Universal Time / Zulu Time / Greenwich Mean Time -- Time is based on 0° longitude running through the Royal Observatory in Greenwich, London, UK. It is the same all year round and is not affected by Daylight Saving Time (aka Summer Time) clock changes. Using UTC allows weather services and others to avoid time zone conversions.
