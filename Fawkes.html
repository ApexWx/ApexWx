NWS State College Hazardous Weather Outlook
Rainfall amounts of 1 to 3 inches are forecast Thursday and Friday with the remnants of Hurricane Zeta. The heaviest amounts will occur to the south of a Johnstown to State College and Williamsport line. The risk of areal and river flooding is low at this time given dry preceding conditions.

Short-Term Outlook: Wednesday - Friday

A weak area of low pressure will track just south of Pennsylvania early this morning with a stalled front lingering over central or northern Virginia today. The remnants of Hurricane Zeta will lift northeast from the Gulf states and ride along this frontal
boundary Thursday, passing south of Pennsylvania late in the day. A strong ridge of high pressure will build into the region Friday.

Note: For US Eastern Time on maps and charts, subtract 4 hours (Daylight Saving Time) or 5 hours (Standard Time) from UTC / Z* timestamp on the maps and charts. Visit timeanddate.com for more time zone conversion information and additional time-related data.

*UTC / Z / GMT = Coordinated Universal Time / Zulu Time / Greenwich Mean Time -- Time is based on 0° longitude running through the Royal Observatory in Greenwich, London, UK. It is the same all year round and is not affected by Daylight Saving Time (aka Summer Time) clock changes. Using UTC allows weather services and others to avoid time zone conversions.
