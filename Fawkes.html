Short-Term Outlook: Sunday -  Tuesday

Hazardous Weather Outlook: A few strong to marginally severe thunderstorms are possible during the afternoon and early evening.

A trough trailing from a low pressure area east of the Great Lakes will cross the region this afternoon with showers and thunderstorms expected. Marginally severe conditions are possible with high-resolution models indicating a line of storms affecting the Lancaster area possible in the early evening period (c. 6 pm - 9 pm).

High pressure will build down from the Great Lakes/Ohio Valley later tonight through early Tuesday. A weak trough of low pressure brings increased cloudiness Tuesday with a slight chance for isolated afternoon showers.

Medium-Range Outlook: Wednesday - Saturday

The middle and end of the week may become more unsettled with an upper trough over the eastern US and a stationary front near the East Coast. The best chance of showers and storms will remain closest to where the stationary boundary sets up, which appears to be our far eastern and southeast counties. By the end of the week, the Bermuda high will slide onto the Atlantic coast funneling moisture up into the Lancaster area. Diurnally driven showers and storms will be possible across region with dew points and temperatures on the rise again.
