Short-Term Outlook: Sunday -  Tuesday

A frontal boundary remains stalled just west of the Lancaster region across central PA. The front will finally track east this afternoon and move away from the Lancaster region tonight. Meanwhile, a cold front will slowly move down from the Great Lakes Monday and slowly drift south across the region Tuesday into Wednesday. This will keep some degree of cloudiness along with chances for showers across the Lower Susquehanna Valley through the period.

Medium-Range Outlook: Wednesday - Saturday

Based on current model data, the remnants of Ida will move along the stalled frontal boundary to the south of Pennsylvania and bring heavy rainfall and the potential for flooding to the region Wednesday into Thursday with current rainfall estimates in the 2-4-inch range. Precipitation is expected to taper off Thursday afternoon as the remnants move east with noticeably cooler, less humid air building into the Lancaster region as high pressure builds down from the Great Lakes through Saturday.
