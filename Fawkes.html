3-Day Outlook: Saturday - Monday

High pressure over Ontario will sag south into Pennsylvania Saturday and Saturday night. A developing area of low pressure over the Gulf Coast will strengthen as it tracks northward along the eastern seaboard Sunday and Sunday night. Snow develops Sunday afternoon with a wintry mix changing to all rain that continues overnight. About 4-6-inches snow are currently expected in Lancaster before precipitation changes to rain overnight. Monday will be breezy with a wintry mix then rain gradually ending and mixing with some snow in the afternoon.

Days 4 to 7: Tuesday - Friday

A surface ridge builds in from the Great Lakes Tuesday. Then, an Alberta Clipper tracks across southern Ontario next Wednesday keeping the bulk of its precipitation north of the border. Southwest flow and associated warm air preceding a trailing cold front should result in a marked warmup with highs Wednesday several degrees above average.

Passage of the trailing cold front is likely to be accompanied by a round of snow or rain showers Wednesday through Thursday. High pressure looks to build in Friday.
