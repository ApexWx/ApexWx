3-Day Outlook: Thursday - Saturday

Low pressure will track from the Southern Plains across the Mid-Mississippi Valley into the Ohio Valley today and Friday. Surface low pressure is expected to track just south of PA Friday night and Saturday. Rain is expected to overspread Lancaster after midnight tonight and continue through Friday afternoon with upwards of an inch of rainfall expected. Rain tapers to showers Friday night continuing into Saturday. A slight chance for isolated thunderstorms is possible early Saturday then again Saturday afternoon. East winds will be gusty Saturday as the low tracks towards the Atlantic. Showers become more scattered Saturday night.

4 to 7 Day Outlook: Sunday - Tuesday

Surface low just south of PA will move offshore Sunday where the upper-trough supporting the surface low will get cut-off from the jet stream and form a closed-upper low off the East Coast. Showers taper off in coverage Sunday with isolated showers by the afternoon. Partly cloudy skies are expected Monday and Tuesday as the upper-low remains blocked offshore. It will retrograde back towards the Southeast Tuesday into Wednesday bringing more in the way of cloud-cover as well as a slight increase in chances for isolated shower activity for Lancaster and vicinity.
