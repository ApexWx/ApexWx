Short-Term Outlook: Thursday - Saturday
  • Excessive Heat Warning in effect today from 12:00 noon until 7:00 PM EDT
  • Strong to severe thunderstorms with gusty winds and small hail are possible late this afternoon or evening.
A Bermuda high pressure centered off the East Coast will bring another hot and humid day to southeastern Pennsylvania and the Lower Susquehanna Valley. Heat index values will top out in as high as 107 degrees this afternoon with an excessive heat warning in effect for the period.

A cold front will approach tonight with showers and thunderstorms possible ahead of the boundary this afternoon and evening. The front will cross the area early Saturday with a chance for showers and thunderstorms.

Behind the front, a much cooler and drier air mass will move into the region with lower humidity levels and temperatures providing relief from the heat wave that has affected the Lancaster area this week.

Medium-Range Outlook: Monday - Thursday

Temperatures next week will be relatively pleasant, as they will stay mostly in the low-to-mid-80s with persistent cloud cover. However, dew points will be in the 60s, contributing to very muggy conditions nearly all week long. There is a chance for daily rain showers and thunderstorms next week with a northward surge of moisture from the remnants of
Fred. While the European and American forecast models are not in agreement currently on whether or not the remnants of Fred will directly affect PA, they do indicate moisture from the system which will produce muggy conditions for the Lancaster region in the mid-to-late week period.
