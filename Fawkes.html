Hazardous Weather Outlook

Heat Advisory in effect from 12 noon through 8 pm EDT today: Heat index values 100° to 104° expected.

Scattered severe thunderstorms with damaging winds 60 mph or greater are possible this afternoon and evening. Isolated flooding is also possible mainly in areas that can experience rapid runoff from heavy rainfall.

Isolated strong to severe thunderstorms with gusty winds are possible Thursday afternoon.

Short-Term Outlook: Wednesday - Friday

High humidity and hot temperatures will lead to high heat indices across the lower Susquehanna Valley today resulting in a heat advisory this afternoon into the early evening. A slowly approaching cold front and disturbances circulating an upper-level high pressure ridge anchored over the south-central US will produce a chance for scattered strong to severe thunderstorms across the region this afternoon and evening. Gusty storm winds are the primary threat. Partly sunny skies today give way to mostly cloudy skies tonight as the front continues to advance. Mostly cloudy to partly sunny skies are expected Thursday as the front slides across Pennsylvania reaching the Lancaster region tomorrow evening. Isolated strong to severe storms are possible Thursday afternoon and evening, mainly along and ahead of the cold front. Partly sunny skies Friday morning become partly cloudy Friday afternoon as high pressure anchored over the Southeast builds into the lower Susquehanna Valley, though a chance for afternoon showers and thunderstorms will persist Friday.

Note: For US Eastern Time on maps and charts, subtract 4 hours (Daylight Saving Time) or 5 hours (Standard Time) from UTC / Z* timestamp on the maps and charts. Visit timeanddate.com for more time zone conversion information and additional time-related data.

*UTC / Z / GMT = Coordinated Universal Time / Zulu Time / Greenwich Mean Time -- Time is based on 0° longitude running through the Royal Observatory in Greenwich, London, UK. It is the same all year round and is not affected by Daylight Saving Time (aka Summer Time) clock changes. Using UTC allows weather services and others to avoid time zone conversions.
