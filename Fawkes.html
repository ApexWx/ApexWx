Short-Term Outlook: Tuesday - Thursday

Cloudy to mostly cloudy skies and mostly dry conditions are expected today as a southeasterly flow aloft draws moisture overhead producing plenty of clouds. A large cyclone centered over the Middle Mississippi Valley region will move northeast into the Ohio Valley  today and tonight with moisture circulating around the system producing rain across the Lower Susquehanna Valley of Pennsylvania by tomorrow morning. Rain is expected to continue through the day and taper to showers Thursday night as the low moves across the Virginias. A final period of steadier rain is expected Friday morning with precipitation winding down in the afternoon as the low moves out to sea and high pressure builds in from the Great Lakes.

*UTC / Z / GMT = Coordinated Universal Time / Zulu Time / Greenwich Mean Time -- Time is based on 0° longitude running through the Royal Observatory in Greenwich, London, UK. It is the same all year round and is not affected by Daylight Saving Time (aka Summer Time) clock changes. Using UTC allows weather services and others to avoid time zone conversions.
