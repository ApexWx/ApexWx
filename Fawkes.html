Apex Wx Three-Day Outlook:  Sunday - Tuesday

 High pressure moves across the Mid-Atlantic today with partly cloudy skies. A deep upper-level trough to the west will move east drawing a warm front across the region tomorrow night into Monday followed by a cold front trailing from a low pressure tacking into Québec Monday into Tuesday. Rain is expected to overspread the Lancaster region tonight with a chance for rain overnight through Monday afternoon as the cold front moves through.

Windy conditions will develop Monday as the front pushes through with breezy conditions overnight into Tuesday with partly cloudy skies developing tomorrow night and continuing into Tuesday afternoon. Clouds increase Tuesday afternoon as a developing coastal low draws moisture into the region. Temperatures will allow for a chance for snow to develop Tuesday night, based on current model data.

Yesterday's high was 53° and the overnight low was 30°.

Lancaster's average high for December 4 is 47° and the average low is 29°.

Apex Wx 4 to 7 Day Outlook: Wednesday - Satureday

A coastal low tracking towards Nova Scotia will bring a chance for snow to Lancaster Wednesday mixing with rain in the afternoon as temperatures rise above freezing. Snow tapers off Wednesday night with partly cloudy skies developing Thursday as high pressure builds down from Canada.

A warm front moving across the region brings a chance for snow Thursday night into early Friday, mixing with some freezing rain Friday morning. Partly sunny skies are expected Friday with increasing clouds Friday night as another low pressure system approaches from the west with a chance for rain showers by Saturday afternoon.
