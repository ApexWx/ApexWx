3-Day Outlook: Thursday - Saturday

Preliminary snowfall totals across the region range from 12-inches in Van Buren with most locations across the County receiving between 7 and 8.5-inches fresh snowfall. Saint Francis and Wallagrass are reporting snowfall totals of 6-inches.

Low pressure will continue to lift northeast away from the Valley over the course of the day with blustery west winds as incoming high pressure and deepening low pressure produce gusty winds today. Areas of blowing snow are possible before winds diminish this evening.

A cold Arctic air mass will move in with fresh snow pack and mostly clear skies allowing temperatures to drop below zero tonight with most of northern Maine in the -5 to -10 degree range with wind chill values in the -15° to -20° range.

Sunny skies are on tap for Christmas Eve with blustery northwest winds across the SJV. Mostly clear skies and breezy northwest winds will accompany Santa's journey across the Valley overnight. Wind chill values will once again fall into the teens below zero overnight.

Christmas Day will be partly cloudy, less breezy, and slightly warmer with highs climbing into the upper teens. Partly cloudy skies are expected overnight into Sunday with a slight chance for snow showers.
