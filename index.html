3-Day Outlook: Sunday- Tuesday

Partly cloudy skies this morning give way to increasing clouds in the afternoon as high pressure moves east and a warm front lifts across the SJV. Snow showers are expected to develop this afternoon with snow showers likely this evening and a chance for snow showers continuing overnight into Monday as a cold front approaches.

The cold front moves through Monday with a chance for snow showers across the Saint John Valley. Light accumulations in the 1-2 inch range are currently expected Monday. Mostly cloudy skies are expected overnight with scattered snow showers for Fort Kent and vicinity as the cold front stalls out in southern New England.

Low pressure tracking towards the region from the southwest Tuesday and lifts the stalled front back northwards as a warm front with a chance for morning snow showers Tuesday with snow likely in the afternoon based on current forecast model data.

Precipitation types and totals are dependent on the low's precise track and frontal location Tuesday-Wednesday; however, current estimates are for up to 12-inches of snow in Fort Kent, Frenchville, and Madawaska, with 8 to 12 inches possible across much of the Valley. Residents of northern Maine should be prepared for the possibility of a significant snow storm or a wintry mix depending on the northern extent of warm air aloft.
