NWS Caribou Hazardous Weather Outlook
Rain continues into this evening. Rainfall totals between 1 to 1.5 inches will fall through tonight Periods of heavy rain could lead to localized flood issues in areas with poor drainage..

The remnants of post-Tropical Cyclone Elsa will move from off the coast of New Jersey across the Gulf of Maine today and into the Maritimes tonight. Current data indicates that around 0.50 to 1.00-inch of rainfall will fall across the Valley over the course of the day. Rain tapers to showers this evening with skies clearing overnight. Partly cloudy skies Saturday become mostly clear Saturday night as high pressure builds across the region.

Clouds increase by Sunday afternoon as a cold front moves across the Valley bring a chance for mainly afternoon showers and thunderstorms to Fort Kent and other Valley locations. Showers continue overnight into early Monday as high pressure spreads across the SJV.
