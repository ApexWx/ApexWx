Apex Wx Three Day Outlook: Wednesday -  Friday

Winter Storm Warning remains in effect until 3 pm this afternoon
Heavy snow. Additional snow accumulations up to two inches for a storm total of 5 to 10 inches. Locations affected include Northeast Aroostook and Northwest Aroostook Counties. Roads, and especially bridges and overpasses, will likely become slick and hazardous. Plan on slippery road conditions.The latest road conditions for Maine can be obtained by going to newengland511.org.

An area of low pressure north of the Gaspé and another in southern New Brunswick will lift north-northeastwards today and tomorrow with steadier snow today tapering to snow showers that linger overnight into early Sunday. Current radar shows most snowfall occurring in eastern portions of the County, though another inch or two of additional snowfall may occur over central portions of Aroostook before tapering to scattered then to isolated snow showers later today and tonight. Areas of blowing snow are possible this afternoon as winds increase thanks to the deepening low pressure over the Maritimes.

Mostly cloudy  skies are expected Sunday as the system continues to drift east-northeastwards while another upper-level system approaches from the west. Surface low pressure associated with the upper-low will track from around Cape Cod towards New Brunswick overnight into Monday then into the Maritimes Monday into Tuesday. However, this system is expected to track further east than the present system confining most snow to the eastern areas of Maine. Much colder air will settle in Monday with daytime highs in the 20s and overnight lows in the teens.

Apex Wx 4 to 7 Day Outlook: Tuesday - Friday

An active weather pattern is expected as more zonal west to east flow sets up across the region. High pressure brings decreased cloudiness Tuesday into early Wednesday. An upper-level trough grazes northern Maine Wednesday with a chance for snow across the SJV. A stronger system is projected to affect the Valley Thursday into Friday with a more significant snow event; however, details are still uncertain this far out.
