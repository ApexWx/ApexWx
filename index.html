3-Day Outlook: Monday - Wednesday

Winter Weather Advisory in effect until 8 pm this evening
Snow and mixed precipitation expected. Total snow accumulations of 4 to 8 inches and ice accumulations of a light glaze expected. Winds gusting as high as 45 mph. Plan on slippery road conditions. Areas  of blowing snow could significantly reduce visibility and cause localized hazardous travel. The hazardous conditions could impact the evening commute. Damage to trees and power lines is possible.

Low pressure over southern NY State at this hour will track to the west of Maine this afternoon. Snow is overspreading the region with the heaviest activity currently in central portions of the state. Warmer air is expected to bring some freezing rain in the late afternoon/early evening; however, water  vapor satellite imagery shows dry air moving northwards which may limit precipitation totals this afternoon. Some heavy snowfall is possible this afternoon along with some gusty winds for a period. However, steadier snowfall will diminish to snow showers tonight before ending early Tuesday. Present estimates are for 3-4-inches along the eastern border with 4-6 inches across much of northern Maine. Snow totals will be affected with the speed of the dry air moving northwards as the low lifts into Canada and continues occluding.

Then our attention turns to another surge of Arctic air arrives for Tuesday with blustery northwest winds and temperatures falling through the day before bottoming out under mostly clear skies with light winds in the -15° to -20° range in Fort Kent. West winds will push wind chill values into the -25 to -35 range or lower at times.

Clouds increase Wednesday as an Alberta clipper approaches with a chance for snow in the afternoon tapering to snow showers that linger overnight into Thursday morning.

Tonight, snow and freezing rain likely with patchy fog in the evening. Snow showers overnight. Chance for precipitation 70% with between 0.10 and 0.25-inch and another 1-inch of snow accumulation possible. Low near 17°. South-southwest winds 8-10 mph.

Days 4 to 7: Thursday - Sunday

High pressure will move east of the region as the day progresses with an Alberta clipper approaching from the west. This will bring some snow Wednesday night that ends Thursday morning. Yet another surge of frigid air arrives for Friday and Saturday with overnight temperatures well below zero. Mostly cloudy skies develop Sunday as a low pressure area near the coast approaches; however, models are inconsistent about its exact track and intensity presently.
