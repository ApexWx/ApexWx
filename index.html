3-Day Outlook: Monday - Wednesday

Partly cloudy skies are expected today and tonight as a much colder air mass moves into the region. A warm front brings increasing clouds Tuesday as high pressure moves east of the region. A chance for snow showers is expected Tuesday afternoon through Wednesday ahead of an approaching cold front from the northwest. Snow showers continue Wednesday night into Thursday morning.

4 to 7 Day Outlook: Thursday - Sunday

A cold front moves through Thursday with a morning snow showers becoming a steadier snow in the afternoon with snow likely overnight into Friday.

Forecast models are uncertain regarding a possible Nor'easter Friday into Saturday. Currently the American GFS and the European models keep an area of low pressure far enough offshore that it will have little impact on the state other than Downeast areas. However, the Canadian CMC Sunday evening model run still has the low closer to shore with a more significant impact on the Valley. The track and strength of this system will determine the impact on the SJV's weather for the late week period, so bears monitoring.

Currently, cloudy skies with a chance for snow is anticipated Friday and Friday night with precipitation tapering off Saturday. Clouds diminish Saturday afternoon and Satrday night before increasing Sunday as another frontal system approaches from the Great Lakes with a chance for snow showers Sunday afternoon.
