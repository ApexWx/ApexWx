3-Day Outlook: Tuesday - Thursday

A cold front and some light snow showers moved across the region this morning. The front will move offshore today as high pressure moves by just north of the region with partly cloudy skies and dry conditions. A weak area of low pressure tracking across Vermont and New Hampshire will move across the Gulf of Maine tonight with mostly cloudy skies and a chance for light snow after midnight tapering to snow showers Wednesday morning. Little, if any, accumulation expected.

Weak high pressure and a low tracking eastwards to the north of the SJV will bring mostly cloudy skies to Fort Kent Wednesday with a chance for scattered morning snow showers with little accumulation expected. A warm front lifting towards the region Wednesday night maintains mostly cloudy skies across the area. Some isolated snow showers are possible after midnight.

The warm front brings overrunning rain showers to the region Thursday as temperatures climb into the middle 40s in Fort Kent and vicinity.  A cold front slides towards the Valley Thursday night and will be poised to cross the region Friday morning with scattered rain showers.

4 to 7 Day Outlook: Friday - Monday

The cold front moves through Friday with a slight chance of rain showers that taper off in the afternoon behind the front. Low pressure will affect the Valley's weather Saturday and Sunday. NWS Caribou in the technical forecast discussion notes that [a]lthough models are in better agreement, the track of the low is still uncertain. However, there has been a trend toward a more southern track which would increase the chances of precipitation remaining mostly in the form of snow across northern areas. Precipitation types/amounts
Saturday into Sunday will be dependent on the eventual track of the low."

Currently, a chance for snow develops Saturday afternoon with snow likely overnight into Sunday morning before tapering to snow showers Sunday afternoon that linger overnight into early Monday before ending. Partly sunny skies develop Monday as high pressure builds into the SJV.
