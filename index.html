Short-Range Outlook: Friday - Sunday

The low pressure that brought rain and snow to the region yesterday will continue to lift northeast away from the region leaving clouds and chilly temperatures in its wake. Meanwhile, high pressure moving from the Midwest into the Mid-Atlantic will bring gradually diminishing clouds tonight into Saturday morning as it builds into the Valley. Mostly clear skies will overspread the region Saturday evening into Sunday morning. Clouds increase Saturday afternoon as a low running along the frontal boundary to the east strengthens and moves back to the west bringing increasing chance for snow Sunday morning that changes to rain Sunday afternoon. Rain and snow showers continue Monday night into Monday morning.

Note: For US Eastern Time on maps and charts, subtract 4 hours (Daylight Saving Time) or 5 hours (Standard Time) from UTC / Z* timestamp on the maps and charts. Visit timeanddate.com for more time zone conversion information and additional time-related data.

*UTC / Z / GMT = Coordinated Universal Time / Zulu Time / Greenwich Mean Time -- Time is based on 0° longitude running through the Royal Observatory in Greenwich, London, UK. It is the same all year round and is not affected by Daylight Saving Time (aka Summer Time) clock changes. Using UTC allows weather services and others to avoid time zone conversions.
