3-Day Outlook: Sunday - Tuesday

Winter Weather Advisory in effect until 1 am Monday
For portions of Central Highlands, Far Eastern, Far Northern and North Woods Maine. Mixed precipitation expected. Storm total snow accumulations of 2 to 5 inches. Ice accumulations of a light glaze. Plan on slippery road conditions.

Low pressure tracking across the Gulf of Maine will move into the Maritimes later today. Snow is likely this morning tapering to snow showers this afternoon before ending this evening as high pressure builds in with clearing skies overnight.

Partly cloudy skies are expected Monday and Monday night with a much colder air mass moving into the region. A warm front brings increasing clouds Tuesday as high pressure moves east of the region. A chance for snow is expected Tuesday night ahead of an approaching cold front from the northwest.

4 to 7 Day Outlook: Wednesday - Saturday

A cold front will move through Wednesday into Thursday with a chance for snow Wednesday and Wednesday night mixing with some rain early Thursday as warmer air moves in aloft. Snow showers are expected through Thursday afternoon before precipitation ends by Thursday evening. A possible Nor'easter is projected by forecast models for late week with a strong low pressure tracking through the Gulf of Maine Friday. A surge of much colder air is expected behind the system next weekend with the Valley in the deep freeze heading into the following week.
