Medium-Range Outlook: Wednesday - Saturday

NWS Caribou Forecast Synopsis
A cold front will cross the area early Wednesday then stall offshore on Thursday while low pressure approaches. The cold front will stall along the coast on Wednesday while high pressure slides into western Quebec. This will push slightly cooler and drier air across the north. A few showers are possible Downeast along the front. Otherwise, the sky will turn out partly to mostly cloudy over the region. The focus Thursday will be on a weak wave of low pressure developing in the southern Appalachians that will track north late Thursday night into Friday. This will bring increasing clouds Thursday followed by a chance for rain, mainly Downeast, Thursday night into Friday. The low and any rain with it will slide east into the Maritimes Friday night as high pressure returns. This will bring a partly sunny and seasonably warm day on Saturday.
