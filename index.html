Short-Term Outlook: Sunday - Tuesday

Clouds and showers are possible this morning into the early afternoon as a weak trough moves across the region behind yesterday's cold front. Currently 0.14-inch precipitation has been recorded by the weather station since midnight with possibly a couple hundredths an inch occurring before high pressure begins building in later today. An anamoulously cool air mass will settle across the Valley for much of the week with temperatures notably cooler than last week and even a chance for some frost, especially Monday night. Daytime highs in Fort Kent will be close to record cool highs today (51°F in 2004).

Partly cloudy skies this evening become mostly clear after midnight. While temperatures will be in the mid-30s tonight, forecast scattered clouds and light winds will likely inhibit frost formation overnight. Partly sunny skies develop Monday as weak instability aloft allows for daytime heating to produce increased cloudiness and a chance for afternoon showers. High pressure over the Ohio Valley Monday night will bring mostly clear and quite cool conditions to the SJV with areas of frost possible across much of northern Maine with overnight lows in the lower-to-middle 30s west-to-east across the region.

Partly cloudy skies are expected Tuesday with increasing chances for showers late in the day into the overnight hours Tuesday night as a weak disturbance circulates around a developing closed upper-low over the Maritimes.

Medium-Range: Wednesday - Saturday

Unsettled weather is expected through much of this period thanks to the upper trough/closed low to the northeast circulating disturbances over the region. Low pressure and a weak cold front affect the region Wednesday into Thursday with increased cloudiness and a chance for showers. High pressure may dry things out a bit Friday before a cold front approaches with showers/thunderstorms Friday evening into the overnight period. Showers linger Saturday under partly cloudy/partly sunny skies.

Special Tropical Weather Outlook

Potential for tropical cyclone formation over the southwestern Gulf of Mexico

Eastern Pacific Tropical Storm Amanda, centered inland over southeastern Guatemala, is forecast weaken into a remnant low or dissipate over Guatemala or southeastern Mexico by tonight.  However, the remnants of Amanda are expected to move northwestward within a broader area of disturbed weather, possibly emerging over the southeastern Bay of Campeche on Monday.  If the remnants  move back over water, environmental conditions appear conducive to support some redevelopment of the system while it moves little through the middle of this week.  Regardless of redevelopment, heavy rainfall is likely to continue over portions of southern Mexico during the next few days.
