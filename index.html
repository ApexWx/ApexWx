3-Day Outlook: Wednesday - Friday

Winter Storm Warning in effect until 7 pm this evening
Snow. Heavy mixed precipitation expected in parts of central Piscataquis County. Total snow accumulations of 6 to 9 inches. Sleet accumulations of around one half of an inch and ice accumulations of around two tenths of an inch expected, mainly across central Piscataquis County. Travel could be very difficult. The hazardous conditions could impact the Wednesday evening commute.

An area of low pressure tracking east across Québec and another low pressure off the East Coast of the US will interact as the day progresses spreading snow across the region. The Atlantic low will eventually absorb the Québec low as it moves into the Maritimes and the Atlantic low tracks east. A surface low around the Bay of Fundy may form later today and track into the Maritimes as the various systems move east. While some mixed precipitation is expected as far north as Houlton/Mars Hill, all snow is expected from Caribou north. Presently, snowfall totals in the 8-12-inch range are expected for areas roughly east of Highway 11 with 6-8 inches in St. Francis/Allagash/Dickey, and 4-6-inches along the western border of the County.

The Atlantic low will deepen while high pressure builds in from the west creating a strong pressure gradient producing gusty west/northwest winds Overnight into Thursday afternoon creating areas of blowing snow through Thursday afternoon.

As the Atlantic low moves away and high pressure takes over, winds will subside Thursday night as very cold Arctic air is drawn into the region. With light winds, clear skies, and fresh snow pack, overnight lows will be in the -5 range in the east to -15° in the west across northern Maine. Wind chills may be as low as 25 below Thursday night.

Friday will be mostly sunny, dry, and cold with highs only in the low teens at best with overnight lows falling back towards zero Friday night.
