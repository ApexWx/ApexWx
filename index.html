3-Day Outlook: Thursday - Saturday

Winter Storm Watch in effect from 5:00 am Saturday, March 12 until 4 am Sunday, March 13: Heavy snow possible. Total snow accumulations of 8 to 16 inches possible. Winds could gust as high as 35 mph Saturday night. Travel could be extremely dangerous. Power outages are possible.

A weak cold front/trough will move across the Valley today with the potential for gusty southwest winds along and ahead of the front. There is a slight chance for some isolated snow showers this morning into the afternoon hours. Partly cloudy skies with light west winds are expected overnight into Friday.

A weak trough behind the front will produce mostly cloudy skies and some light snow across the SJV tomorrow with an inch or less of accumulation in Fort Kent and surrounding locations. Then, all eyes turn to a significant weather maker for the weekend.

Low pressure will track towards New England from the Mid-Atlantic region Friday night then track from around Cape Cod Saturday morning into New Brunswick by Saturday night. Plenty of moisture is associated with this system and, based on its current projected track, will allow cold air to remain in place over northern Maine. This creates a recipe for abundant snowfall with current estimates in the 12-18 inch range for most of northern Maine. Lesser amounts are expected from Caribou/Presque Isle south as enough warm air may infiltrate north to allow rain to mix in Saturday afternoon lowering overall accumulations in those area southwards.

The low will intensify as it moves into the Maritimes with windy conditions Saturday producing areas of blowing snow, scattered power outages, and dangerous travel conditions. Wrap around precipitation will continue Saturday night into Sunday morning before tapering to snow showers and ending Sunday afternoon as the system moves away from the region. All-in-all, this has the potential to be a major late-season storm with moderate to major impacts across most of northern Maine. Area residents are advised to plan accordingly and avoid unnecessary travel for this event.
