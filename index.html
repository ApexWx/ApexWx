3-Day Outlook: Sunday - Tuesday
An upper-level low will move across the region today with afternoon showers and isolated thunderstorms. With cool air aloft producing low freezing levels, small hail is possible with any stronger thunderstorms that develop this afternoon. Precipitation tapers off around midnight as the upper-low moves east with high pressure building into the Valley (also helping to steer Tropical Storm Alex to the east into the open Atlantic). A trough axis behind the departing low will keep some moisture across the region early Monday with mostly cloudy skies becoming partly cloudy in the morning then mostly clear in the afternoon as drier air moves over the SJV. Clouds begin to increase Monday night as a warm front moves towards the region ahead of low pressure and its trailing cold front north of the Great Lakes that will be tracking east. Partly sunny skies with a chance for showers develop Tuesday afternoon with showers likely Tuesday night into Wednesday as the front approaches the region.
