3-Day Outlook: Tuesday - Thursday

An occluded front with a weak area of low pressure tracking along it will move across the Valley this morning into the afternoon hours. Snow may briefly build in this morning before changing to all rain as the morning progresses. Precipitation tapers to showers this afternoon ending by the evening as the front moves east.

High pressure build across the SJV tonight into Wednesday with partly cloudy to mostly clear skies Tuesday night becoming partly cloudy Wednesday as a warm front lifts towards the region producing a chance for rain and snow showers Wednesday night.

An occluded front approaches Thursday with showers likely by the afternoon continuing overnight into Friday morning as the occlusion move across the SJV by Friday morning.

NWS Caribou Ice Movement / Flood Outlook: "Fragmentation, openings, and significant ice rot continues on the Saint John and Allagash Rivers with flowing ice chunks and open water over a majority of the Aroostook River. River flows continue to increase due to recent rainfall and snowmelt, which is increasing pressure on remaining ice sheets. Ice is flushing out over the Aroostook River and starting to let go over portions of the Saint John and Allagash Rivers as well. An ice jam risk continues, mainly across the Saint John and Allagash Rivers due to jamming over locations where ice is thicker or it encounters islands and/or substantial bends in the river.

Runoff will increase again today with around a tenth to quarter of an inch of precipitation and temperatures in the 40s causing snowmelt to resume. The latest snow survey data from Monday indicates snow water equivalent of 5 to 9 inches and snow depth of 18 to 29 inches from Winterville north through Allagash, indicating that there is still a significant amount of water left to melt across the Saint John and Allagash River basins. Further south along Route 11, snow water equivalents were less than 5 inches with snow depths less than 15 inches. High temperatures could reach the upper 40s to 50s across much of the area Wednesday and Thursday. Additional jams appear unlikely along the Aroostook River, but continued river level rises are likely and ice jams remain possible across the Allagash and Saint John Rivers this week."

For information on the region's Spring Flood Outlook, see https://forecast.weather.gov/product.php?site=car&issuedby=CAR&product=ESF.
