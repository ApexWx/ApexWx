3-Day Outlook: Thursday - Saturday

The coldest temperatures of the season are upon us today and tomorrow. Canadian high pressure building east will bring a surge of Arctic air back into the Valley on brisk northwest winds, Today's high will occur this morning with temperatures falling over the course of the day. Overnight lows will bottom out in the vicinity of -15° east to -25° west across northern Maine (winds will be near calm until morning, so wind chills will not become an issue until then).

Temperatures will rise towards 0° in Fort Kent tomorrow then plummet tomorrow night with the broad area of high pressure nearly overhead allowing temperatures to drop to around -25° east and -30° west across the Crown. Sheltered valleys could see even colder temperatures, with exposed or elevated locations seeing less frigid temperatures if any breeze occurs. Regardless, it will be bitterly cold for the next couple of nights.

Saturday will see a heatwave in Fort Kent with temperatures rising towards the lower teens followed by a less chilly night with temperatures only falling into the -10s. An approaching cold front may provide some clouds with down-welling infrared from them warming the air a bit.

Days 4 to 7: Sunday - Wednesday

A cold front moves through Sunday bringing another round of Arctic air to the region, through not as strong as the Thursday-Friday blast. The ridge will cross the region Monday night and move east Tuesday. A clipper system is expected to affect the Valley Tuesday and Wednesday bringing a chance for snow to the region.
