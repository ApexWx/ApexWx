Short-Term Outlook: Monday - Wednesday

NWS Caribou Hazardous Weather Outlook
A cold front will slowly approach from the west later this afternoon and tonight. Thunderstorms are expected to develop later this afternoon and into this evening. A few thunderstorms may contain gusty winds, small hail, and heavy rainfall.

Skies will cloud up in Fort Kent and other areas of the SJV as a cold front approaches from the west. The front will move through Maine Tuesday and into the Maritimes Wednesday where it will encounter the remains of Claudette tracking off Nova Scotia. Showers and scattered thunderstorms are expected to spread across the region this afternoon with isolated to scattered strong to severe storms possible. Showers and thunderstorms will continue into the evening hours before thunderstorms wane. A chance for showers continues overnight into Tuesday morning.

Tuesday will feature plenty of clouds and showers along with a chance for afternoon thunderstorms as the cold front works its way across the SJV. With plenty of moisture being injected aloft from Claudette, locally heavy downpours are possible. The front will move through Tuesday afternoon with cooler, drier air beginning to build in behind. Showers are likely overnight; however, dew points will drop back into the 40s as the drier air mass settles over the region.

Mostly sunny to partly cloudy skies overspread the Valley Wednesday as high pressure dominates the area's weather. Temperatures will be comfortable with lower humidity levels and a chilly overnight period as mostly clear skies allow temperatures to fall into the mid-40s across the SJV.
