Short-Term Outlook: Saturday -  Monday

High pressure centered northwest of the Valley this morning will cross the region today then move southeast of the area Sunday. A warm front is expected to approach the SJV Sunday bringing increasing chances for showers activity to the Valley by the afternoon. Humidity levels will rise Sunday night into Monday ahead of a cold front moving towards the region from the west.

The cold front will move through Monday with showers likely along with afternoon thunderstorms. Based on current forecast model data, a few stronger storms are possible with locally heavy downpours and gusty storm winds. Current model consensus indicates up to half-an-inch of rainfall in Fort Kent and surrounding areas. Precipitation will linger Monday night with decreasing cloudiness as high pressure builds in from the Great Lakes.

Medium-Range Outlook: Tuesday - Friday

High pressure builds into the region Tuesday and Wednesday with partly cloudy to mostly clear skies along with dry and less humid conditions. The remnants of Ida may cross the waters Thursday, though the exact track still varies on various models. Currently, just a slight chance for isolated afternoon showers is expected Thursday and again on Friday as high pressure moves from the Great Lakes into New England.
