Short-Term Outlook: Saturday -  Monday

A prefrontal trough is bringing some showers to the Valley this morning. A cold front will move through the Valley this afternoon with showers likely in the afternoon along with a chance for scattered afternoon thunderstorms across the area.

High-resolution forecast models indicate a line of storms may develop after noon and progress across the SJV in the 12 - 4 pm period. Isolated strong to severe storms are possible, mainly in eastern portions of the County, but morning cloud cover and daytime heating will also factor into the strength of any storms that develop. Heavy downpours and gusty storm winds are the main threats from any stronger storms with localized street flooding possible.

Behind the front, humidity levels will drop off and skies clear tonight as high pressure builds in from the Great Lakes with cooler temperatures and dry conditions for Sunday. Valley residents will have plenty of sunshine, highs in the low 70s and much less humidity. Lows will range from the low to mid/upper 50s west to east across northern Maine Sunday night.

Monday will be slightly warmer as southwest flow sets up across the region as high pressure moves into the Gulf then drifts east of Cape Cod Monday night. Clear skies and dry conditions are expected into Tuesday.
