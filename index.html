Short-Term Outlook: Thursday -  Saturday

Chances for showers increase Thursday morning as an area of low pressure tracks from the southern Great Lakes towards Maine. Showers overspreading the SJV in the late morning period and continuing through the afternoon into the overnight period.

A chance for showers continues Friday morning into the afternoon along with a chance for scattered thunderstorms as a weakening cold front approaches from the west. Precipitation winds down in the evening as the front dissipates and daytime heating is lost.

Clouds increase Saturday morning as a warm front moves across the region. Chances for showers increase by the afternoon along with another round of scattered thunderstorms. A chance for showers and some isolated thunderstorms continues through the overnight period into Sunday morning.

Medium-Range Outlook: Sunday -  Wednesday
Sunday will have a mix of clouds and sun across the SJV in the morning with partly cloudy skies in the afternoon. Any lingering showers are expected to end in the afternoon as high pressure over the Mid-Atlantic builds into the region.

This ridge will bring warmer and more humid air to the Valley with daytime highs approaching daily records early next week (Fort Kent reached 91° on June 7 in 1976 and 84° on June 8, 1941)). A slight chance for afternoon showers and isolated thunderstorms fired up by daytime heating are expected Monday.

Tuesday will feature an approaching cold front which will introduce a chance for isolated showers and afternoon thunderstorms to the Valley; however, timing is uncertain this far out.

Isolated showers are possible Tuesday night into Wednesday morning. Cooler, less humid air will build into the region Wednesday as Canadian high pressure builds down from the northwest.
