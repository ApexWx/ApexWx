National Weather Service Cold Water Safety Message
The warm air temperatures today may cause people to underestimate the dangers of the cold water temperatures which are currently only around 50 on the lakes and in the lower to mid 60s on the rivers.

The cold water temperatures can quickly cause hypothermia to anyone immersed in the water. According to the Maine Warden Service, when the water temperature is below 60 degrees, the average submerged person could lose dexterity within minutes and be unable to accomplish simple tasks. Anyone on small boats, canoes or kayaks should plan accordingly if recreating this weekend and use extreme caution to avoid this threat. Paddle smart from the start and always wear your life jacket!

Gusty winds are expected today with gusts up to 25 mph from the southwest. If boating on large bodies of water, these strong winds can increase the likelihood of overturning your boat,canoe or kayak due to increased wave heights.

Short-Term:  Friday - Sunday

Another warm and humid day is on tap for the Saint John Valley as high pressure to the east continues to channel warm, moist southerly air into the region. The upper-ridge that controlled the region's weather much of the past few days is breaking down and this will allow a strong cold front trailing from a low-pressure area in Québec to approach the region tonight bringing a chance for showers and thunderstorms, with some strong-to-severe storms possible later today into the overnight hours. Plenty of moisture aloft and training storms could lead to locally heavy downpours with any storms that develop along and ahead of the cold front. The Storm Prediction Center in Norman, Oklahoma has placed much of northern and western Maine at a marginal risk for isolated severe thunderstorms. Heavy rainfall and strong storm wind gusts are the primary threats based on current forecast atmospheric profile data. Storms are most likely this evening through the midnight period, though a chance for thunderstorms will remain through Saturday morning.

A much cooler air mass will move in behind the front with temperatures some 30 degrees cooler by Sunday as an upper-trough settles over the Northeastern US. Partly cloudy-to-partly sunny skies are expected Sturday along with a chance for showers. Showers persist overnight into Sunday afternoon as a secondary cold front moves through the Valley. High pressure will build into the region Sunday with skies becoming partly cloudy by the afternoon and precipitation coming to an end. Partly cloudy skies and quite chilly temperatures are expected Sunday night with lows dipping into the middle 30s for Fort Kent and Frenchville.
