Short-Term Outlook: Monday - Wednesday

Hazardous Weather Outlook: Some showers or thunderstorms that develop this afternoon may contain heavy rainfall.

A cold front will slowly move from central Maine towards the coast today. Most activity associated with this front will occur along and south of it affecting interior and Downeast Maine for the most part. Fort Kent and vicinity will have a slight chance for showers through the afternoon period with partly sunny skies. Partly cloudy skies tonight with some isolated showers along with a chance for fog as winds drop to near calm overnight.

Tuesday will feature partly sunny skies and a slight chance for an isolated shower as the front remains stalled out south of the Valley. The front will lift back northwards as a warm front Tuesday night with increasing chances for showers. A cold front will move through the region Wednesday bringing the likelihood of afternoon showers and a chance for isolated strong-to-severe thunderstorms to the SJV. Showers and thunderstorms are expected to continue overnight into Thursday.

Medium-Range Outlook: Thursday - Sunday

Moisture circulating around the periphery of a subtropical ridge of high pressure in the Atlantic will continue to dominate the Valley's weather through the period. Based on current forecast model data, a low pressure area will move along a quasi-stationary frontal boundary Thursday producing showers across the area.

The front moves to the coast by Friday with a continued chance for showers across the SJV. High pressure builds across the region Saturday with a chance for showers. Another low pressure area and associated cold front will approach the Valley Sunday into Monday with a chance for shower activity across the region.
