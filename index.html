3-Day Outlook: Tuesday - Thursday

Wind Chill Warning in effect until 12 noon / Wind Chill Advisory in effect from 12 noon - 6 pm this evening
Dangerously cold wind chills expected this morning. Wind chills as low as 35 to 45 below zero this morning. Very cold wind chills expected this afternoon. Wind chills as low as 20 to 30 below zero expected this afternoon. The dangerously cold wind chills could cause frostbite on exposed skin in as little as 10 minutes.

The personal weather station reached -14.2° at 7:26 am this morning.

A cold ridge of Arctic air will move across the SJV today with the coldest temperatures of the season so far with overnight lows in Fort Kent in the -20 to -25 degree range. High pressure moves east of the region tomorrow with moderating temperatures. A warm front approaches Wednesday with a chance for snow showers and plenty of clouds. An associated cold front moves through Thursday with a chance for snow showers in the afternoon and evening. Currently, light accumulations are expected.

Days 4 to 7: Friday- Monday

Thursday's cold front will bring another surge of Arctic air into the SJV; however, this is not expected to be quite as cold as the current outbreak. An oceanic low well off the Mid-Atlantic coast brings some increased cloudiness Friday and Saturday along with a chance for some snow showers both days. The low moves away Sunday with partly cloudy skies as high pressure builds in. Current forecast model runs indicate a potential Nor'easter MLK Day into Tuesday; however, there are some discrepancies in the track (closer to Maine or further east in the Maritimes) as well as timing which will bear monitoring.
