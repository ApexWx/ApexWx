Short-Term Outlook: Saturday -  Monday

The remnant low of former tropical cyclone Ida will gradually drift east-southeastwards in the Maritimes this afternoon with skies becoming mostly clear across Fort Kent and the SJV. Radiational cooling overnight will allow temperatures to drop into the low 40s to upper 30s across the region. Given near calm winds, some lower reading may be possible in some locations. Some patchy frost may occur in low lying and river valley areas.

Clouds increase Sunday across the region over the course of the day as an occluded front approaches with showers overspreading Fort Kent and vicinity in the evening with rain overnight tapering to rain showers Monday morning. Partly sunny to mostly cloudy skies are expected Labor Day with a chance for showers as the front moves through. Showers taper off in frequency Monday night as a ridge of high pressure builds in behind the departing front.

Medium-Range Outlook: Tuesday - Friday

Showers linger Tuesday as high pressure briefly builds across the SJV. Another front approaches that will produce another round of shower activity to Fort Kent and vicinity Wednesday through Friday. High pressure builds in Saturday with a chance for mainly afternoon showers possible in Fort Kent and vicinity.
