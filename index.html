Apex Wx Three Day Outlook: Sunday - Tuesday

Wind Advisory in effect until 1 pm ET
West winds 15 to 25 mph with gusts up to 45 to 50 mph. Gusty winds will blow around unsecured objects. Tree limbs could be blown down and isolated power outages may
result.

Deepening low pressure tracking towards Labrador will bring gusty winds in the 25 to 35 mph range in Fort Kent and vicinity with some higher gusts possible. High pressure will build in today with partly sunny skies across the SJV. Winds will remain breezy tonight then diminish tomorrow as the pressure gradient eases.

Mostly cloudy skies develop Monday afternoon as a cold front approaches from the north/northwest. Partly cloudy skies develop Tuesday as high pressure builds in from the west on gusty northwest winds.

Apex Wx 4 to 7 Day Outlook: Wednesday - Saturday

High pressure brings partly cloudy skies Wednesday morning with increasing clouds in the afternoon as another western-tracking low pressure area develops over the Plains and tracks across the western Great Lakes towards James Bay in Canada.

This system will bring another round of snow changing to a wintry mix Thursday morning into the afternoon followed by rain Thursday afternoon tapering to showers Thursday night and ending as rain/snow showers Friday morning. High pressure builds back in Friday with partly sunny skies over the region. An off-shore low may bring a chance for widespread snow across the state Saturday; however, track and timing of this system are not certain at this time due to forecast model variances.
