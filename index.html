3-Day Outlook: Friday - Sunday

Clouds increase this morning as an area of low pressure over the Mid-Atlantic tracks offshore with high pressure to our north keeping the system suppressed to the south. Partly cloudy skies this morning become overcast this evening with some light snow likely in the afternoon with little accumulation, if any, expected. Central and southern portions of the state will see the greatest snowfall with the interior and coastal portions of the Downeast with some areas along the coast seeing up to a foot of accumulation.

Any precipitation ends this evening with clearing skies and temperatures diving back into the teens below zero for most of northern Maine with the far northwest towards Estcourt Station potentially reaching the 20s below.

High pressure will build into the region Saturday with partly cloudy skies and dry conditions. Clouds increase Saturday night as a weak cold front drops from the northwest with a slight chance for scattered snow showers after midnight.

A cold front moves across the Valley Sunday with mostly cloudy skies and a chance for scattered snow showers along with snow squalls as the front moves through creating difficult driving conditions. Brisk northwest winds will create areas of blowing snow Sunday night as high pressure builds in from the west.
