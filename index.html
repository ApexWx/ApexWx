Short-Term Outlook: Thursday -  Saturday

NWS Caribou Hazardous Weather Outlook
Moderate to locally heavy rain is expected tonight over far Northern Maine. Any potential flooding should be localized and relatively minor.

A warm front will lift towards the Valley today with showers transitioning to a steady rainfall overnight as moisture from the remnants of TS Elsa are drawn into the region. Elsa's remnants will track along the Downeast coast Friday ahead of a cold front pushing through the state. Rainfall will be the primary feature from these systems with around 1.5 to 3-inches of rainfall west to east across northern Maine. Given the drought conditions that have been in place for several weeks now, the rainfall will help mitigate the dry conditions.

Rainfall will taper off Friday night with patchy fog and clearing skies overspreading the SJV. High pressure will bring mostly sunny skies Saturday afternoon and mostly clear conditions Saturday night.
