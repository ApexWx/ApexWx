3-Day Outlook: Sunday - Tuesday

An upper-level low will move across the SJV today with a chance for rain showers this morning and showers likely this afternoon into the evening. A temperature inversion along with calm winds has allowed areas of dense fog to form across much of the region this morning with a dense fog advisory in effect until 10 am. Visibility of one-quarter mile or less will create hazardous driving conditions this morning.

Cloudy skies persist into the overnight hours before the upper-low moves east with decreasing cloudiness by Monday morning. A chance for snow showers is expected overnight as temperatures drop to near/below freezing.

High pressure builds in Monday with partly cloudy to mostly clear skies developing by the afternoon. Partly cloudy skies overnight Monday become increasingly cloudy Tuesday morning as a fast-moving area of low pressure and associated cold/occluded front slides across the Valley. Early morning snow showers give way to rain as the morning progresses with rain likely through the afternoon before tapering to showers in the evening. Rain mixes with snow overnight into Wednesday as temperatures cool to near or just below freezing overnight.

NWS Caribou Ice Movement / Flood Outlook: "Fragmentation, openings, and significant ice rot continues on the St. John, Allagash, and Aroostook Rivers. River flows continue to increase due to recent rainfall and snowmelt, which is increasing pressure on existing ice sheets across the aforementioned rivers. Ice is starting to let go over portions of these rivers and jamming over locations where ice is thicker or it encounters islands and/or substantial bends in the river. Snowmelt rates will taper tonight as temperatures fall near or below freezing, but continued river level rises and ice movement is expected through next week from recent rain and snowmelt."

For information on the region's Spring Flood Outlook, see https://forecast.weather.gov/product.php?site=car&issuedby=CAR&product=ESF.
