3-Day Outlook: Friday - Sunday

Hazardous Weather Outlook: Low pressure is expected to move across the region later New Year`s Day into Sunday. This system has the potential to bring snow, sleet and freezing to the region. Snowfall accumulations of between 3 to 5 inches along with around one tenth of an inch of ice is possible over the weekend. Please monitor the latest forecasts for the upcoming weekend.

High pressure will build across the region today with low-level moisture keeping skies cloudy. A warm front will lift across the Valley tonight with a chance for snow showers and freezing drizzle through the overnight hours. A cold front will approach from the west Saturday with a low pressure area tracking along the frontal boundary from the southwest.

Steadier snowfall is expected to overspread the region by Saturday afternoon with some freezing rain mixing in during the afternoon for a period. Snow is likely Saturday night before tapering off Sunday morning. Snowfall totals for most locations look to be in the  4-6-inch range with localized amounts in the 6-8-inch range. An Arctic air mass will move in Sunday on blustery northwest winds with cold and dry conditions.

4 to 7 Day Outlook: Monday - Thursday

A much colder Arctic air mass moves into the Valley for early next week with partly cloudy skies and highs only in the teens Monday and Tuesday. High pressure will move east into the Atlantic with moderating temperatures midweek. Low pressure tracking east from the Great Lakes will bring a chance for snow to the Valley by Thursday.
