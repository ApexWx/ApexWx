Short-Term Outlook: Tuesday - Thursday

A cold front stalled out west to east over central Maine this morning and will eventually advance to the coast this afternoon. Meanwhile, another cold front to our northwest will sag southwards Wednesday into Thursday with an area of low pressure tracking along the frontal boundary.

Showers and a slight chance for thunderstorms are expected across the SJV Wednesday as this system moves through. Showers continue Wednesday night into early Thursday. A chance for showers remains across the region Thursday as the cold front slowly moves towards the coast.

Medium-Range Outlook: Friday - Monday

An upper-level trough will channel moisture circulating around the periphery of a subtropical high pressure ridge in the Atlantic across the Valley throughout the period.

Mostly cloudy skies are expected Friday with a chance for showers. Partly sunny skies develop Saturday as surface high pressure builds in through Sunday afternoon with a chance for showers Saturday and Sunday. Clouds increase Sunday night into Monday as a cold front approaches with a chance for showers.
