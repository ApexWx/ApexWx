Short-Term Outlook: Wednesday - Friday

High pressure over western Québec will build into the Valley today and center over the region tonight. The ridge will move east of the region tomorrow with a low pressure area approaching from the west with a cold front. This system will move across the SJV Thursday night into Friday with rain likely along with a slight chance for isolated thunderstorms Friday afternoon. Rain tapers to showers behind the front Friday night.

Friday night, partly cloudy with a 50% chance for showers. Rainfall totals less than 0.10-inch expected. Low near 50° with northwest winds 5-10 mph.

Medium-Range Outlook: Saturday - Tuesday

An upper-level trough will keep a chance for scattered showers across the Valley Saturday. A cold front approaches Sunday with a chance for more widespread showers across the area. Large ridge of high pressure over the West will keep a persistent trough over the East with a chance for showers continuing Monday and Tuesday.
