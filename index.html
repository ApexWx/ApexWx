Short-Term Outlook: Tuesday -  Thursday

Surface high pressure centered over western Ontario will build east into the region today with mostly sunny skies this morning into the afternoon. Partly cloudy skies develop later today as a weak upper trough moves across the region.

Partly sunny skies are expected Wednesday with partly cloudy skies Wednesday night as the upper trough remains near the region.

Partly sunny skies Thursday morning becoming increasingly cloudy by the afternoon as the remnant low of Ida track south of Cape Cod towards the waters off Nova Scotia. Some wrap around moisture brings some scattered showers to the Valley Thursday afternoon with showers continuing Thursday night into Friday.

Downeast, heavy rainfall is expected along the coast and may penetrate as far as the Central Highlands. Washington and Hancock counties may also experience heavy rainfall Thursday night into Friday.

Medium-Range Outlook: Friday - Monday

An upper-level trough keeps partly sunny skies and a chance for showers across the Valley Friday. A ridge of high pressure over the Middle Atlantic brings partly cloudy skies and dry  conditions Saturday.

Partly sunny skies with increased chances for showers are expected Sunday as a cold front approaches from the west with a chance for widespread showers Monday as the cold front moves across the SJV.
