Short-Term Outlook: Tuesday - Thursday

NWS Caribou Hazardous Weather Outlook
Afternoon thunderstorms are again expected today, especially over East Central and interior Downeast areas. Thunderstorms will contain  heavy rainfall and a few storms may also contain hail and gusty winds as well.

The PWS recorded 0.22-inch rainfall in the last 24-hours.

Cloudy skies this morning will become mostly cloudy this afternoon as a cold front slowly crosses the SJV. With plenty of moisture being injected aloft from Claudette, locally heavy downpours are possible. Thunderstorms are also possible this afternoon; however, today's storms are expected to be generally non-severe though stronger cells could feature gusty winds and small hail in addition to heavy downpours. Most of this activity is expected to be focused in eastern portions of the County towards the New Brunswick border.

The front will move into the Maritimes this evening with cooler, drier air beginning to build in behind lowering the humidity levels significantly.

Mostly sunny to partly cloudy skies overspread the Valley Wednesday as high pressure to the west dominates the area's weather. Temperatures will be comfortable with lower humidity levels and a chilly overnight period is in store tomorrow night as mostly clear skies and light winds allow temperatures to fall into the lower-to-middle 40s west to east across the the North Woods and SJV.

High pressure will move east of the area Thursday with mostly sunny skies continuing and warmer temperatures as south/southwesterly flow sets up behind the ridge pushing daytime highs into the mid-to-upper 70s for most Valley locations. Mostly clear skies and mid-50s lows are expected Thursday night.

Medium-Range Outlook: Friday - Monday

Partly cloudy skies continue Friday as high pressure moves east. There is a slight chance for showers after midnight Friday night as a low pressure area and cold front begins to push towards the region from the Great Lakes.

Saturday will feature partly sunny skies and a chance for showers through the day and a chance for afternoon thunderstorms. Humidity increases Saturday night with showers likely and a slight chance for scattered thunderstorms.

Cloudy skies are expected Sunday and Monday with showers likely along with a chance for scattered afternoon thunderstorms both days as the cold front slowly approaches the region. Sunday will be rather muggy with slightly less humid conditions Monday.
