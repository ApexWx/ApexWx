Temperatures climb into the low teens today in Fort Kent and vicinity with increasing clouds in the afternoon as a warm front lifts across the region ahead of an approaching cold front that will move through Friday. Little moisture is associated with the front with around 1 to 2 inches of snow expected in Fort Kent and surrounding locations.

Attention turns to a strengthening low pressure system moving towards the Canadian Maritimes in the western Atlantic Saturday. While models are still not consistent with regards to the precise track and strength of the low, a track further east across the Maritimes will result in less snow for the region while a track further west closer to Downeast Maine will have a greater impact with regards to snow, wind, etc. on the state.

Any shift east or west in the low's track will affect the snowfall totals across the state, though.
