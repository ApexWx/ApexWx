3-Day Outlook: Saturday - Monday

An upper low will approach today and support the development of a surface low that will track across southern New England into the Maine late today into Sunday. Any morning rain/snow showers today will change to a steadier rain in the afternoon with the approaching low. Rain will persist through the evening hours before tapering to rain/snow showers overnight in the wake of the low.

Rain showers are expected Sunday morning through the afternoon with a chance for rain and snow showers Sunday evening. Rain/snow showers linger overnight before ending early Monday morning. High pressure will build in Monday with decreasing clouds and partly cloudy skies in the afternoon into the overnight hours.

NWS Caribou notes in their hydrology discussion that the "latest snow survey from early this past week shows snow depths of 10-30 inches across the St. John & Aroostook River basins. The snow water equivalent in this snowpack ranges from 5 to 12 inches. Much of the snowpack except in the North Woods is near or is "ripe" at this time. "Ripe" means any rainfall will not be absorbed and will result in water release from the snowpack. Yesterday's rainfall, and up to an additional 0.25-inch rainfall tonight, combined with temperatures through the weekend in the mid 40s to near 50F will likely result in ice movement. As the ice rots, breaks up expect an increased potential of ice jams especially on the Aroostook River starting this weekend into next week. Expect only isolated movement on the Allagash & St. John Rivers starting this weekend into next week due to much thicker ice on those two rivers."
