Winter Storm Warning in effect from 7:00 PM this evening until 10 AM Friday morning. Rain changing to heavy mixed precipitation expected. South/southwest flow in the warm sector ahead of the front will allow rain to overspread the region today continuing into the early evening.

The cold front will slowly move through the Valley tonight and reach the coast tomorrow morning. Rain will change to snow with a period of freezing rain in the evening as colder air moves back into the region behind the cold front. Snowfall totals tonight look to be in the 6-inch range in Fort Kent. Gusty north winds behind the front will create areas of blowing snow overnight.

Snow is likely Friday morning as an area of low pressure tracking along the front shifts to the northeast of the region allowing precipitation to wind down to snow showers Friday afternoon before ending by the late afternoon period. Another couple of inches of snow are expected to accumulate in Fort Kent for a total snowfall in the 8-inch range with up to 11-inches possible on the high end.

High pressure will briefly build in Friday night into early Saturday with partly cloudy skies and cold conditions overnight. Clouds increase Saturday as a fast-moving clipper low tracks towards the SJV from the Great Lakes and moves through Saturday night with some light snow accumulations for the Crown of Maine.
