Apex Wx Three-Day Outlook: Thursday - Saturday

Winter Storm Warning in effect from 4 pm this afternoon through 7 pm Saturday evening
Heavy snow expected. Total snow accumulations of 5 to 8 inches with highest amounts over the higher terrain. Locations affected include Northeast Aroostook, Northwest Aroostook, Northern Piscataquis and Northern Somerset Counties. Plan on slippery road conditions. The hazardous conditions could impact the evening commute and post holiday travel. Gusty winds could bring down tree branches.

An upper-level trough is sliding eastwards towards the region this morning. Surface low pressure associated with the system continues to spread rain/snow across the Valley this morning. A secondary low is expected to develop over the Gulf as the morning progresses. This low will pivot into New Brunswick tonight and track towards the Gaspé by Saturday afternoon. Precipitation changes to all snow tonight with snow continuing through Saturday afternoon. As the low deepens winds will become gusty allowing for blowing snow to develop over the course of the day Saturday. Snow tapers to snow showers Saturday night with precipitation tapering off early Sunday morning. The surface low meanders near the mouth of the Saint Lawrence Saturday night then lifts northwards into Labrador Sunday. Snowfall totals from the event look to be in the 4-6-inch range for much of northern Maine with 6-8 inches in the North Woods as well as along the Maine-New Brunswick border in a band from around Grand Isle south.

Partly sunny skies are expected Sunday as the upper-low and trough drift northeastwards while another system approaches from the west (discussed in the 4-7 Day Outlook below). Quite cold temperatures will settle across Fort Kent and surrounding areas with lows Sunday night dipping into the low 10s.

Apex Wx 4 to 7 Day Outlook: Monday - Thursday

An upper-trough and associated surface low pressure will move across southern New England Sunday night and into the Gulf early Monday. However, it is expected to move into the Maritimes further east than the current system with any snowfall occurring along the eastern Maine border and only a slight chance for snow for most of the region. The remainder of the period will feature zonal flow from the west with several weak disturbances keeping partly sunny skies and a chance for isolated to scattered snow showers across the Valley through Thursday.
