3-Day Outlook: Monday - Wednesday

Wind Chill Advisory in effect from 9 pm tonight until 7 pm Tuesday
Very cold wind chills expected. Wind chills as low as 30 below zero expected.

A very cold ridge of Arctic air will build across the Valley today and tomorrow. Highs today will only be in the single digits above with overnight lows falling into the -15 to -20 degree range. Wind chill values will drop to -30° at times. Tomorrow will be partly cloudy and bitterly cold with highs in the -5° to -10° range across northern Maine. Tuesday night lows will drop into the -15° to -20° range once again with wind chills in the -20 to-25 degree range. High pressure moves east Wednesday with high temperatures moderating into the teens above zero. A cold front brings clouds and a chance for snow showers Wednesday afternoon.

Tonight, partly cloudy to mostly clear and quite cold with a low near -15°. Northwest winds at 6-8 mph. Wind chill values -25° to -30°.

Wednesday, January 12

Wednesday night, mostly cloudy and cold with a low near 5°. 10% chance for snow showers. South winds 0-7 mph.

Days 4 to 7: Thursday - Sunday

A cold front will move through Thursday with snow showers in the morning hours. Temperatures drop off once again as another surge of Arctic air moves into the SJV; however, this is not expected to be as cold as the early week outbreak. An oceanic low well off the Mid-Atlantic coast brings some increased cloudiness Friday and Saturday along with a chance for some snow both days. The low moves away Sunday with partly cloudy skies and a slight chance for snow showers Sunday.
