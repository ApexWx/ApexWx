3-Day Outlook: Saturday - Monday

Winter weather advisory in effect from 10 am this morning until 10 am Sunday: Mixed precipitation expected. Total snow accumulations of 1 to 4 inches, sleet accumulations up to one half of an inch and ice accumulations of one-tenth to one-third of an inch expected. Highest amounts of snow, sleet, and freezing rain in the Saint John Valley. Power outages and tree damage are possible due to the ice. Travel could be very difficult.

Low pressure tracking towards the region from the southern Great Lakes will bring precipitation in the form of snow this morning mixing with sleet and rain in the evening as warmer air moves in aloft. A sleet / freezing rain mix is expected overnight into Sunday morning before tapering to rain showers that continue until Sunday evening.

With regards to icing, NWS Caribou's technical discussion notes, "upper air model soundings indicate that the prevailing surface winds will be easterly, keeping temps right around freezing, especially in the north. As the warm air aloft pushes northward throughout the day, snow will start to transition to sleet and freezing rain. Since the past few days have had high temperatures well above freezing with plentiful snowmelt, confidence in ice accumulations has decreased. In addition, recent temperature models indicate that surface temps will hover near freezing today and upper level temps will be slightly warmer, which also decreases confidence in ice accumulation."

Colder air moving back over the region behind the departing low pressure system will transition precipitation back to snow showers that linger Sunday night into Monday. An upper-level trough associated with the system will move across the SJV Monday with scattered snow showers across the area through the afternoon. Partly cloudy skies with a slight chance for snow showers is anticipated Monday night. Northwest winds will pick up as the low intensifies to the east of the region.

4 to 7 Day Outlook: Tuesday - Friday

Windy conditions are expected Tuesday as the pressure gradient increases as high pressure builds into the region from the west and interacts with the strong low pressure area to the east. Mostly cloudy skies with scattered snow showers are expected Tuesday with partly cloudy skies and continued breezy conditions Wednesday. A low pressure system will affect the Valley Thursday - Friday; however, forecast models are still not in agreement with the system's track as well as any secondary low pressure development along the coast -- both of which will influence the precipitation types/amounts across the SJV. Currently, snow Thursday afternoon mixing with rain overnight then back to snow Friday morning before changing to a rain/snow shower mix later Friday.
