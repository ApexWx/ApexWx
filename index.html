3-Day Outlook: Monday - Wednesday

A weak cold front will move through the region this morning into the afternoon followed by an upper-level trough tonight. Incoming high pressure and the departing weekend low will create gusty northwest winds across the SJV today. Isolated to scattered snow showers will also be possible over the course of the day, particularly during the afternoon with a few bands of heavier snow showers possible.

The upper-trough moves east into the Maritimes Tuesday with mostly cloudy skies, continued blustery northwest winds, and a chance for isolated snow showers through the afternoon. Partly cloudy skies develop Tuesday night with northwest winds subsiding across the region.

Partly cloudy skies are expected Wednesday as high pressure remains north of the region. High clouds will increase later in the day as strong low pressure over the Middle Mississippi Valley tracks towards the Great Lakes.

4 to 7 Day Outlook: Thursday - Sunday

Forecast models are still not in agreement with the Great Lake low's track as well as any secondary low pressure development along the coast -- both of which will influence the precipitation types/amounts across the SJV. Canadian and European models are showing a potential coastal low while other models have a more westerly track for the low. Models also show cold air damming in place initially, which would favor more frozen than liquid precipitation.

Based on current data, snow is expected to overspread the Valley Thursday afternoon and continue overnight into Friday. Snow continues Friday morning before tapering to rain/snow showers in the afternoon as warmer air moves over the region. Precipitation becomes more isolated Friday night into Saturday morning when it is expected to taper off.

An upper-level low/trough will remain across the region Saturday and Sunday with mostly cloudy skies and a chance for morning snow showers and afternoon rain/snow showers Sunday.
