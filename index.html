3-Day Outlook: Monday - Wednesday

High pressure builds in today with partly cloudy skies. Partly cloudy skies overnight tonight become increasingly cloudy early Tuesday morning as a fast-moving area of low pressure tracks across south/central Maine and an associated occluded front slides across the Valley. Early morning snow gives way to rain as the morning progresses with rain likely through the afternoon before ending. Little/no snow accumulation is anticipated from this system.

High pressure build across the SJV Tuesday night into Wednesday with partly cloudy to mostly clear skies Tuesday night becoming partly cloudy Wednesday as a warm front lifts towards the region producing a slight chance for precipitation Wednesday and Wednesday night.

NWS Caribou Ice Movement / Flood Outlook: "Fragmentation, openings, and significant ice rot continues on the St. John, Allagash, and Aroostook Rivers. River flows continue to increase due to recent rainfall and snowmelt, which is increasing pressure on existing ice sheets across the aforementioned rivers. Ice is starting to let go over portions of these rivers and jamming over locations where ice is thicker or it encounters islands and/or substantial bends in the river. Snowmelt rates will taper again tonight as temperatures fall below freezing. However, runoff will increase again Tuesday with around a tenth to quarter of an inch of precipitation and temperatures in the 40s. High temperatures could reach the 50s across much of the area Wednesday and Thursday. Continued river level rises and ice jams are expected, particularly across the Aroostook River over the next 24 hours, then expanding to include the Allagash and Saint John Rivers by mid-to-late week."

For information on the region's Spring Flood Outlook, see https://forecast.weather.gov/product.php?site=car&issuedby=CAR&product=ESF.
