Short-Term Outlook: Monday - Wednesday

NWS Caribou Hazardous Weather Outlook
Isolated to scattered thunderstorms will develop this afternoon. Some storms may be strong, with gusty winds.

A warm front will cross the Valley today followed by a cold front tonight. Showers along with a chance for scattered afternoon thunderstorms today will be followed by showers and areas of fog tonight. Partly cloudy skies develop Tuesday as high pressure gradually builds into the region with a slight chance for isolated afternoon showers. Wednesday will feature partly cloudy skies and dry conditions as high pressure builds across the SJV.

Medium-Range Outlook: Thursday - Sunday

High pressure will move east Thursday as a cold front approaches from the west. The cold front will bring a chance for showers and thunderstorms along and ahead of the front Thursday night as it moves through. An upper-level trough will move in behind the front with a continued chance for primarily afternoon showers and thunderstorms Friday into Friday night. The trough moves east later Saturday followed by another cold front Sunday with showers and afternoon/evening thunderstorms possible across the region.
