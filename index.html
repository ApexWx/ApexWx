Short-Term Outlook: Saturday -  Monday

High pressure to the northeast will bring partly sunny skies today with enough moisture aloft to produce a chance for isolated showers and possibly a thunderstorm or two this afternoon. Any precipitation winds down this evening with the loss of heating. Patchy fog is possible overnight into early Sunday with light winds expected across the region.

Partly sunny skies are expected Sunday as surface high pressure and its associated upper-level ridge shift further northeast of the region. It will be a quiet day weather-wise with only a slight chance for isolated showers in Fort Kent and the SJV.

Clouds increase Sunday night as Henri is expected to make landfall near Long Island and move into southern New England where it will rapidly weaken. The remnants of Henri will move east into the Gulf of Maine as it continues to weaken.

For the SJV increasing chances for showers are expected late Sunday night continuing through Monday as Henri and its remnants track across New England. Enough instability is expected aloft to produce a chance for isolated afternoon thunderstorms Monday with shower activity continuing into Monday night.

Medium-Range Outlook: Tuesday - Friday

Mostly cloudy skies with a chance for showers are expected Tuesday. High pressure builds in Wednesday and Wednesday night with mostly sunny to partly cloudy skies. A slight chance for isolated showers is expected Wednesday.

Scattered showers develop Wednesday night as a cold front approaches with partly sunny skies and the likelihood of showers Thursday afternoon along with some isolated thunderstorms.

A chance for showers and isolated thunderstorms continue Thursday night with decreasing clouds and scattered showers expected Friday behind the cold front. Humidity levels will remain rather muggy through much of the week with notably lower humidity behind the front Friday.
