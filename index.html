3-Day Outlook: Tuesday - Thursday

Hazardous Weather Outlook: Accumulating snowfall of 2 to 4 inches is expected for most of the region during the day today as weak low pressure from the west crosses the region. Snowfall along the immediate Downeast coast will be 1 to 2 inches where snow may mix with rain during the afternoon.

A clipper low will bring snow to the SJV today with up to 3-4 inches of fresh snowfall for in  central and western Aroostook with 2-3 inches expected along the eastern border of the County based on current model data. Mostly cloudy skies are expected this evening with snowfall ending. Partly cloudy skies develop after midnight for a few hours. Clouds increase Wednesday morning as a low pressure approaches from the west with another round of snowfall across the Valley in the Wednesday night through Friday morning as the system slowly crosses the region.

4 to 7 Day Outlook: Friday - Monday

Snow is expected to taper off Friday afternoon as the region will be between departing and approaching systems. Mostly cloudy to partly sunny skies are expected Saturday with increasing chances for snow in the afternoon. Forecast model variability is high for the weekend; however, presently, low pressure moving up from the Mid-Atlantic/Ohio Valley will bring a chance for snow Saturday afternoon through Monday morning, though precipitation type, amount, and duration are still uncertain at this time and will depend on the strength and track of the system. Partly sunny/cloudy skies are expected to develop Monday afternoon with much colder Arctic air moving into the Valley.
