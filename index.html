Three-Day Outlook: Monday - Wednesday

A cold front will sweep across the SJV from the west this morning into the afternoon hours. Mostly cloudy skies and windy conditions with a slight chance for some showers will be the primary markers of the front's passage. High terrain and open areas will experience wind gusts as high as 35 mph this morning into the afternoon.

Tonight, high pressure over the northern Plains will build in with mostly clear skies and lighter winds as the pressure gradient diminishes. Partly cloudy skies are expected Tuesday as high pressure builds across the region. A weak upper-level trough will move through tomorrow with a chance for some afternoon showers with little rainfall expected.

Wednesday will remain partly cloudy and cool with highs topping out in the mid-40s in Fort Kent and vicinity. Partly cloudy skies and cold conditions are expected Wednesday night with temperatures falling into the 20s.
