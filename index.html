Short-Term Outlook: Friday - Sunday
Thus far 0.04-inch rainfall has been recorded since midnight at the time of this posting. Low pressure is moving along the Maine coast this morning with a cold front poised to the west-northwest. The cold front will move through the SJV this afternoon with showers possible through the period. Showers continue this evening into the overnight hours with some patchy fog possible after midnight.

An upper-level trough ahead of a ridge of high pressure centered over the Ohio Valley will maintain a chance for showers into Saturday afternoon. Skies become partly cloudy Saturday night as the high pressure ridge brings decreased cloudiness and dry conditions overnight into Sunday.

Clouds increase Sunday morning as a warm front lifts across the region followed by a cold front Monday with showers likely Sunday night into Monday.
