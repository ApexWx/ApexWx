Short-Term:  Thursday - Saturday
High-pressure will weaken and move east over the course of the day with return flow around the back of the high channeling warm, moist air into the region. Dew points in the 60s and air temperatures in the upper 80s to low 90s will make for a sultry summer-like day in the County. Fort Kent will be near or possibly exceeding the record high for this date of 90°F set back in 1978. A weak trough aloft may allow for some isolated to scattered thunderstorms this afternoon; however, any that develop are expected to be less robust than yesterday afternoon's thunderstorms. Nonetheless, enough moisture aloft could allow for some heavy downpours with any storms that do develop.

A cold front advancing from the west will slowly approach tonight and tomorrow with increasing chances for showers and afternoon/evening thunderstorms. There will be plenty of moisture aloft, so any storms that develop could produced locally heavy rainfall. Showers continue overnight as the cold front is expected to move through Québec to the western Maine border by early Saturday keeping a slight chance for thunderstorms across the Valley overnight into early Saturday. Showers are expected Saturday as the cold front moves through the region and the upper-ridge that brought this week's heat will be replaced by a deep upper-level trough that will bring notably cooler temperatures to the Valley by Sunday.

Medium-Range: Sunday - Wednesday

The first week of June will be dominated by a deep trough over the Northeast with much cooler temperatures moving in Sunday as high-pressure centered over the Great Lakes builds east into New England. Skies become partly cloudy Sunday afternoon with a chance for shower activity over the course of the day. High-pressure will slide over the Ohio Valley early next week with partly cloudy/mostly clear skies, drier conditions, and below normal temperatures for the SJV. An area of low-pressure brings a chance for showers Tuesday night and Wednesday.
