Short-Term Outlook: Saturday -  Monday

Last evening's storms brought 0.08-inch of rainfall to the PWS. High pressure is building in this morning with partly cloudy skies across the SJV. The cold front that pushed through the Valley late yesterday has stalled out across central Maine and will lift back north later today as a warm front. Overnight, dew points will climb into the lower 60s in Fort Kent making it feel rather muggy.

Mostly sunny skies are on tap Monday with temperature climbing into the lower 90s in Fort Kent and vicinity placing the record high  of 91° for June 7 (set in 1976) in jeopardy. Dew points in the low 60s will make it feel somewhat uncomfortable as well. Caution should be used on area lakes and rivers since water temperatures are currently only in the mid-50s on the lakes  and in the mid-60s on the rivers. Hypothermia can quickly occur in the water despite the warm air temperatures. Winds will also be gusty Monday afternoon, so caution should be used when operating any watercraft to avoid capsizing (and do not forget the life jacket). While there is a slight chance for a pop-up shower or storm, the day is expected to be hot and humid otherwise.

Monday night will be mostly clear, warm, and humid with overnight lows dipping into the mid-to-upper 60s.  Tuesday looks to be another day when high temperatures will be challenged as high pressure continues to build across the region: Fort Kent's record high of 84° was set in June 8, 1941.

A weak trough will move across the region Tuesday with a slight chance for isolated showers/thunderstorms; however, there is little in the way of moisture associated with this system. A notable feature will be lower dew points and less humid conditions.

Medium-Range Outlook: Wednesday - Saturday

A cold front is expected to move through Tuesday into Wednesday with a chance for scattered showers across the SJV. Canadian high pressure will build down from the Hudson Bay bringing cooler, drier conditions. Indeed, sheltered locations could see temperatures drop into the 30s with a chance for patchy frost Thursday night. A weak disturbance moving through Friday will bring a chance for isolated showers during the afternoon and overnight hours. Another ridge of Canadian high pressure will be building down from the northwest Saturday with a weak disturbance bringing a chance for widely scattered showers Saturday afternoon.
